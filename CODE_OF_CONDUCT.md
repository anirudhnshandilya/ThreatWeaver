# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and maintainers of the ThreatWeaver community, pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or technical background.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contribute to a positive environment include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
* Focusing on what is best for the project and the wider community
* Helping new contributors understand the project and contribution process
* Discussing technical disagreements professionally and respectfully

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

* Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory comments
* Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
* Personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission
* Deliberately disrupting project discussions or development
* Submitting malicious code, intentionally vulnerable code, or misleading security findings
* Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Security Research Conduct

ThreatWeaver is a cybersecurity project. Contributors must use the project responsibly and ethically.

Contributors must not use ThreatWeaver to:

* Access systems without authorization
* Exploit infrastructure belonging to another person or organization
* Collect, expose, or misuse sensitive information
* Distribute malware or destructive payloads
* Facilitate unlawful activity
* Misrepresent security findings or research results

Security testing must only be performed against systems that you own or have explicit permission to test.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behaviour.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation by contributors whose behaviour is considered inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

* GitHub issues
* Pull requests
* Discussions
* Code reviews
* Documentation
* Community channels
* Project-related public communication

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the ThreatWeaver project in public spaces.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported privately to the project maintainer.

**Project maintainer:** Anirudh N Shandilya

Please avoid reporting sensitive conduct matters through a public GitHub issue.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as reasonably possible.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may apply the following actions depending on the nature, severity, and frequency of a violation.

### 1. Correction

A private or public warning explaining the inappropriate behaviour and how it should be corrected.

### 2. Warning

A formal warning with consequences for continued behaviour. The contributor may be asked to avoid interaction with specific individuals for a defined period.

### 3. Temporary Restriction

A temporary restriction from participating in project discussions, contributions, or community spaces.

### 4. Permanent Ban

Permanent removal from participation in the project and its community spaces.
